I’m a final year PhD candidate in Competition Law at University College London. My doctoral research asks how powerful companies harm the effective structure of competition. It draws from theoretical ecology and systems theory to propose a new ‘ontology’ of competition, and uses it to provide fresh interpretation of exclusionary abuse of dominance under Article 102 TFEU.

Outside of my PhD, I work on a variety of topics related to EU competition law and digital market regulation. You can find my publications here. I also serve as a graduate lecturer at UCL Laws.

I hold a master’s degree in Politics and Technology from the Technical University Munich and a bachelor’s degree in Computer Science from The University of Manchester. I previously worked for six years as a software engineer at Google, mostly in the security and privacy space.
